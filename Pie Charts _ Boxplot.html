Google Sheets - Benefits and Drawbacks

There are several key benefits and drawbacks to Google Sheets:

Benefits
  • Google Sheets allows multiple users to access the same spreadsheet at the same time, and is great for collaboration for that reason.
  • Because it is an internet-based tool, Google Sheets are free to use, up to 15GB of data, apart from the cost of the internet connection itself.
  • Google Sheets allow for integration of your data and visualizations with other Google products like Google Slides, Google Docs, Google Analytics, and more.
  • You can access your sheets from any computer with an internet connection, while Excel files are usually only accessible on the computer on which they were created.
  • There is a set of built-in revision history options.
Drawbacks
  • Its speed depends on the speed of your internet connection.
  • There arenât as many formulas available in Google Sheets as there are in Excel.
  • There arenât as many data visualization options in Google Sheets as there are in Excel.
  • Formatting is not as highly customizable in Google Sheets as it is in Excel.

Excel - Benefits and Drawbacks

On the other hand, there are also a number of key benefits and drawbacks to Excel:

Benefits
  • Excel spreadsheets have access to the full range of functions and formulas.
  • Formatting functionality is fully customizable.
  • There are far more data visualization options in Excel than there are in Google Sheets.
  • Excel is usually much faster than Google Sheets because its speed does not depend on the speed of your internet connection.
  • For more advanced users, Excel allows for easier automation because of VBA, easier integration with R, Python, and SQL, and other sophisticated features.
